Stretch for Relief

The life of a student isn't as simple as we think it is. The more they study, the more stress they accumulate from doing so.
Not only that but they have lives and other responsibilities to tend outside of University. Stretching benefits us in many ways.
From helping blood circulate around the body, to relieving us of any stress or tension in our muscles.
Below are a some basic stretches to help reduce your stress levels or to increase your flexibility.

These stretches are to be performed for 60 seconds on each arm. They can be performed whilst seated or standing.
20 second rest in between set/Prep for next stretch.
Equipment needed: Mat (optional)and a towel or cloth.

Overhead Tricep Stretch



Beginning one hand at a time. Bring your hand straight up over your head.
Use your muscles in your shoulder to pull your arm back behind you as much as you can. This a really good way to increase
flexibility in your shoulder whilst doing so in a controlled manner.

Shoulder Crossbody Stretch



This stretches your upper back muscles but primarily focuses on your shoulder muscles.
Extend your arm across your body towards your opposite shoulder. Using your other hand, reach out and grab your elbow.
You should feel the stretch along the back of your shoulder with your arm extended.

Rear Arm Raises



This will stretch the front of your shoulders. Using your rear deltoids, pull your arms up towards the rear.
Doesn't matter how hard you pull backwards, hold your arms up as high as you can towards the rear and hold.

Legs Up the Wall



This stretch/pose will help loosen pressure on your lower back and is just as simple and straight-forward as it's name.
Lie down on your back with your legs extended against the wall, as shown in the illustration.
As you do so, have a towel or folded cloth ready to place underneath your lower back, NOT your tailbone.
Now take a few deep, long breathes, this will open up and release the pressure in your lower back.
After completing your first set of 60 seconds, rest lying flat on your back for 20 seconds before repeating this stretch for the last time.

These stretches will focus on relieving pressure found on your lower body.
60 seconds each leg. 20 seconds rest/prep for next stretch.

Standing Quad Stretch

Standing, keep your own balance or if needed, use a table top or wall as support.
Curl your heel up as close as you can to your glutes using just your hamstrings.
Using the same hand as your leg lifted, grab and hold your foot to maximise the stretch

Inside Thigh Stretch

Spread your feet twice as wide as your shoulder width. Bend your right knee outwards. Use the muscles on the outside
of your thigh to push your hip down, tilting your hips towards the leg which is straight(left leg in this case).
After 60 seconds on one leg, repeat for the other leg.

Butterfly Stretch



This stretch will greatly stretch your groin and loosen your hips.
Sitting on the floor, bring feet together and pull them in as close as possible.
Use the muscles on the outside of your thigh to pull your knees down towards the ground.
Keep your back straight. You should feel the stretch once again in your groin area.

Deep Glute Stretch



Lay down on your back. Cross one leg over the top of the opposite knee.
Pull that same knee beneath the crossed leg in as tight as you can, towards your chin.
The stretch should be felt in the glute of your leg which is crossed.
